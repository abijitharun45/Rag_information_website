About US Irinjalakuda Diocese was established in the year 1978, and His Excellency Mar James Pazhayattil took over the reins as the first Bishop of this Diocese. Irinjalakuda Diocese is doing a excellent service meeting the needs of Education, Health and Social responsibilities. Considering the demands on the necessity of value based higher technical education, the people of the Diocese decided to establish an Engineering college to mark The Episcopal Silver Jubilee Year of His Excellency Bishop Mar James Pazhayattil and Silver Jubilee of the formation of the Diocese of Irinjalakuda . The Irinjalakuda Diocesan Educational Trust was formed and registered on 23-07-2001 with Reg. No: 138/IV 2001 at Irinjalakuda registration office with His Excellency Mar James Pazhayattil as Chairman, Rev. Msgr. Sebastian Ezhekadan as President, Rev. Fr. Joseph Thekkethala as Secretary, Rev.Fr. Pius Chirapanath as Finance Officer. With land holding about 40 acres , the Trust submitted the application form for establishing Sahrdaya College of Engineering and Technology at Kodakara, to the Government of Kerala, University of Calicut and AICTE , New Delhi through the Regional Office. By the Grace of God, the AICTE, New Delhi in its letter dated 06-06-2002, sanctioned the establishment of Sahrdaya College at Kodakara, to run the four B. Tech Degree courses leading to Electronics and Communication Engineering, Computer Science and Engineering, Biomedical Engineering and Biotechnology (Engineering) with an intake of 60 students in each of the courses from the academic year 2002-2003The Government of Kerala included the Sahrdaya College as one of the engineering colleges to which the students have to be admitted by the Commissioner of Entrance Examination from the year 2002-2003 in the four branches sanctioned by the AICTE. On 21st November 2001 His Excellency Mar James Pazhayattil laid the foundation stone for central building of Sahrdaya College. Architects Mayphils from Ernakulam prepared the master plan for the college and detail plan for the individual buildings. On 22/02/2002 His Excellency Mar James Pazhayattil laid the foundation stone for the workshop and hostel building in the campus. In a short span of seven months the central building, workshop and hostel block were completed in all respects. All the 4 class rooms of the first batch were equipped with Networked Computers with LCD projector for Multimedia Presentations, Teak wood furniture, imported green glass Magnetic chalk boards etc. The laboratories required for first year in Physics and Chemistry departments and the workshop required in Mechanical, Electrical & Electronics and Civil engineering departments were fully equipped. A drawing hall containing modern adjustable drawing boards was also made available for use. An advanced Computer Lab with 60 networked Pentium-4 terminals was established. A language lab to train the students in English, Hindi, French, German, Arabic and Japanese languages was also established with necessary audio support from Linguaphone Institute Ltd, London. A vast reference library which can accommodate 150 students at a time and issue library with text books and reference books was established with a well qualified librarian and necessary supporting staff. Library is being digitalized. Well-qualified teaching staff varies from master‚Äôs degree to doctorate degree conversant in using power point presentations were recruited. Internet facilities are provided in the college. Buses were provided for transporting staff and students. On 22/6/2002, the college building was blessed by His Excellency Mar James Pazhayattil, Honourable M.L.A.‚Äôs viz., Adv. K.P. Viswanathan MLA, Mr. P.P. George MLA, Mr. T.U. Radhakrishnan MLA, Ms. Savithri Lakshmanan, MLA, Mr. M.K. Paulson MLA, Mr. P.R. Prasadan, Village Panchayat President, Rev. Priests, Sisters and Parish Representatives, well wishers and other dignitaries were present on the occasion. The college office started functioning in the new premises with Executive Director, Principal and office staff on 23-09-2002.

The purpose of this tour policy is to establish guidelines and procedures governing the planning, organization, execution, and evaluation of tours and field trips. This policy ensures that these activities align with the institution's educational objectives, prioritize safety, and adhere to legal and ethical standards.

OBJECTIVES

The tour policy encompasses various aspects related to tours and field trips, including but not limited to:

  • Planning and Approval
  • Safety and Risk Management
  • Participant Eligibility and Consent
  • Budgeting and Financing
  • Code of Conduct and Expectations
  • Evaluation and Feedback

GENERAL GUIDELINES

  1. Tour is encouraged only for final year students, preferably during the Odd semester.
  2. The class representatives or two members (One boy & One girl) nominated by the students, with the approval of the class advisor and HOD, will act as tour coordinators.

  7. Students under suspension are not eligible for the educational tour.
  8. Student tour coordinators must submit a detailed report, including financial statements, routed through the HoD to the Principal within one week of return.
  9. At least two faculty advisors must accompany the students. In case of any inconvenience, they must hand over their charge to another faculty member, which is the responsibility of the faculty advisors.

  11. Two parents (Father & Mother) must be part of the tour, and their names should be submitted with the tour schedule to the principal. A lady parent must accompany students if female students are part of the tour.
  12. Students suffering from health problems will not be permitted to attend the tour/visit.
  13. A consent letter from parents is compulsory for all students attending tour programs.
  14. The expenses of accompanying parents and faculty members must be covered by the students.
  15. Advance payments (ticket booking for train journey, bus, accommodation, or any other payment) should not be made before obtaining permission from the Principal and Executive Director.
  16. The educational tour can be proposed for 5 days (4 days and 5 nights), starting Monday evening and ending Saturday morning. The fifth day can be clubbed with the tour to manage the railway timetable.
  17. Mode of Journey:
    • Rail journey is preferred for all places if possible.
    • Night journeys by bus are not permitted.
  18. The tour must start from college; no students are permitted to join after departure from college.
  19. Smoking, liquor consumption, and similar activities are strictly prohibited during the tour program.
  20. These rules are intended for the final year tour, which should be completed by March 11th, 2023.
  21. A route map with a detailed itinerary showing places of visit, departure/arrival times, places of stay, and lists of students, faculty, and parents must be submitted to the Faculty Tour Coordinator from the Department for approval to avail of Railway Concession.

Purchase Committee Policy

The Purchase Committee meeting is conducted at the beginning of each financial year or as needed. Consolidated budget requirements from concerned HODs, approved by the Director, are submitted to the accounts section by January each year. These budgets are then reviewed by the Finance Officer and approved by the Executive Director before being presented to the Board of Trustees for final approval by April.

Purchase Process

For purchase requisitions (PR/02) over Rs. 25,000 or for capital items, quotations are collected, and comparative statements (PR/03) are prepared with departmental recommendations. Following authorization by the Director, these are approved by the Executive Director, with budget confirmation from the accounts department. If necessary, negotiations may occur with the recommended supplier, and purchase orders (PR/04) are issued after approval from the Finance Officer and Executive Director.

For purchases below this limit, departments can make cash purchases with advance taken against the Director's recommendation, settled monthly. Online purchases require special permission from the Finance Officer and Executive Director.

Budget Management

If the budget is unavailable, funds may be reallocated from other departmental heads with approval. Upon receipt of items, departments certify bills for acceptance, and stock is updated in separate registers (ST 01/ST 02) maintained by the Admin Executive. Payments are made according to agreed terms.

Contracts and Supplies

For housekeeping and maintenance, annual contracts are established with agencies, with proposals solicited one month before contract expiry. Stationery requirements are estimated and purchased from approved suppliers, with a stock register maintained for consumables.

Committee Responsibilities

The Purchase Committee ensures compliance with laws and college procurement policies, mediates disputes, upholds ethical procurement practices, and promotes sustainable procurement. The committee continuously reviews and improves procurement processes while providing guidance to college departments.

Staff Training Policy

This policy encourages faculty members at Sahrdaya College of Engineering (SCET) to enhance their skills through various training and development opportunities while ensuring minimal disruption to regular classes.

Key Points

Training Programs Faculty can attend short-term training, seminars, workshops, and conferences, with a maximum of two per year during semester breaks. Participation in programs must be in their area of specialization and from reputable institutions. Induction Program All newly joined staff must attend a mandatory induction program. Reporting and Documentation Certificates and course reports must be submitted to the Training and Placement Officer (TPO) for duty leave approval. Course materials received should be handed over to the Department Library. Financial Provisions Faculty members are not eligible for travel allowances (TA/DA) or registration fees for attending training programs. Duty Leave Duty leave is granted for the duration of the course and travel upon submission of the course report. Industrial Training Faculty are encouraged to pursue industrial training during semester breaks (maximum of 6 days per year) with duty leave provided. Knowledge Sharing Faculty attending programs must conduct a sharing session with their department colleagues. Research and Outreach Activities Faculty are encouraged to present papers at conferences, publish in SCI/Scopus indexed journals, and engage in outreach activities, with duty leave available (no registration fees or TA/DA). Internal Development Programs Faculty Development Programs may be organized during semester breaks or holidays.
